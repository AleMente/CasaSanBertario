Location & territory

Cassino: crossroads
of history, nature and industry

For those considering tourist rental, the territory generates multi-segment demand, stable in every season.

Cultural and religious tourism
The Abbey of Montecassino, founded in the 6th century by Saint Benedict, draws tens of thousands of international visitors each year. Historical memory tourism adds flows from Germany, Poland, the United States and Australia linked to the 1943–44 Battles of Cassino.
Academic mobility and business travel
The University of Cassino generates structural demand throughout the academic year. The provincial industrial district — including nationally significant manufacturing groups — produces a continuous flow of professionals and managers on assignment with a high average spend.
Nature and winter tourism
A few kilometres away lies the Valle del Comino and the Abruzzo, Lazio and Molise National Park — one of Europe's most important protected areas — home to the Apennine wolf and the Marsican brown bear. In winter, Roccaraso and the Mainarde an hour away extend the catchment area to ski tourism.
